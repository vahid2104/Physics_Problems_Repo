Problem 8 – Relative Motion

Visualization and animation of two moving bodies in different reference frames

Given velocities

vA = (3, 1),     vB = (1, -2)

Step 1 – Relative velocity of A with respect to B

vA/B = vA - vB = (3,1) - (1,-2) = (2,3)

So the velocity of body A relative to body B is:

vA/B = (2, 3)

Step 2 – Direction of the relative motion

The direction is the same as the vector (2,3). This means A moves relative to B:

  • 2 units to the right,
  • 3 units upward.

The angle with the positive x-axis is:

θ = arctan(3/2) ≈ 56.3°

Step 3 – Meaning of the three reference frames

  • Origin frame: both A and B move with their own velocities.
  • Frame attached to A: A is fixed, and B moves relative to A with velocity vB/A = vB - vA = (-2,-3).
  • Frame attached to B: B is fixed, and A moves relative to B with velocity vA/B = (2,3).

In A’s frame

  • A stays at rest.
  • B moves left and downward with velocity (-2,-3).

In B’s frame

  • B stays at rest.
  • A moves right and upward with velocity (2,3).

Final answer

vA/B = (2,3),     direction angle = arctan(3/2) ≈ 56.3°
