# Problem 1 – Uniform and uniformly accelerated motion

Given the equation of motion

$$
x(t)=x_0+v_0 t+\frac{1}{2}at^2
$$

we need to determine:

* the velocity ( v(t) ) and the acceleration ( a(t) ),
* for ( x_0=0 ), ( v_0=5\ \text{m/s} ), ( a=-2\ \text{m/s}^2 ):

  * the stopping time,
  * the maximum velocity depending on time and the sign of the acceleration,
  * the maximum displacement,
* the graphs of ( x(t) ), ( v(t) ), and ( a(t) ).

---

## 1. Velocity and acceleration

The velocity is the derivative of position with respect to time:

$$
v(t)=\frac{dx}{dt}
$$

Differentiate

$$
x(t)=x_0+v_0 t+\frac{1}{2}at^2
$$

Thus,

$$
v(t)=v_0+at
$$

The acceleration is the derivative of velocity with respect to time:

$$
a(t)=\frac{dv}{dt}
$$

Since

$$
v(t)=v_0+at
$$

we obtain

$$
a(t)=a
$$

Therefore:

$$
v(t)=v_0+at
$$

$$
a(t)=a
$$

---

## 2. Substitute the given values

We are given

$$
x_0=0,\qquad v_0=5\ \text{m/s},\qquad a=-2\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

Substitute these values into the equation of motion:

$$
x(t)=0+5t+\frac{1}{2}(-2)t^2
$$

$$
x(t)=5t-t^2
$$

Now substitute into the velocity formula:

$$
v(t)=v_0+at
$$

$$
v(t)=5-2t
$$

The acceleration is constant:

$$
a(t)=-2\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

So the motion is described by:

$$
x(t)=5t-t^2
$$

$$
v(t)=5-2t
$$

$$
a(t)=-2\ \text{m/s}^2
$$

---

## 3. Stopping time

The object stops when its velocity becomes zero.

So we solve

$$
v(t)=0
$$

Substitute ( v(t)=5-2t ):

$$
5-2t=0
$$

$$
2t=5
$$

$$
t=\frac{5}{2}=2.5\ \text{s}
$$

Therefore, the stopping time is

$$
t_{\text{stop}}=2.5\ \text{s}
$$

## 4. Maximum velocity depending on time and the sign of acceleration

The velocity function is

$$
v(t)=5-2t
$$

This is a linear function with negative slope because

$$
a=-2<0
$$

So the velocity decreases as time increases.

At ( t=0 ):

$$
v(0)=5
$$

Thus the maximum velocity is

$$
v_{\max}=5\ \text{m/s}
$$

and it occurs at

$$
t=0
$$

### General rule

* If ( a>0 ), velocity increases with time.
* If ( a<0 ), velocity decreases with time.
* If ( a=0 ), velocity remains constant.

In this problem, since ( a<0 ), the maximum velocity is the initial velocity.

## 5. Maximum displacement

The maximum displacement occurs when the object stops, that is when

$$
t=2.5\ \text{s}
$$

Use the position function

$$
x(t)=5t-t^2
$$

Substitute ( t=2.5 ):

$$
x(2.5)=5(2.5)-(2.5)^2
$$

$$
x(2.5)=12.5-6.25
$$

$$
x(2.5)=6.25\ \text{m}
$$

Therefore, the maximum displacement is

$$
x_{\max}=6.25\ \text{m}
$$
