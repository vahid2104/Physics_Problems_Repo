Problem 2 – Energy of a Harmonic Oscillator

Step-by-step explanation of the natural frequency, the total energy, and the displacement where the kinetic energy is 50% of the total energy.

Given Data

m = 1 kg
k = 100 N/m
x(0) = 2 m
v(0) = 1 m/s

1. Natural Frequency

For a mass–spring oscillator: ω₀ = √(k / m)
Substitute the values: ω₀ = √(100 / 1) ω₀ = √100 ω₀ = 10 rad/s
Ordinary frequency: f = ω₀ / (2π) f = 10 / (2π) f ≈ 1.59 Hz
Natural angular frequency: ω₀ = 10 rad/s
Natural frequency: f ≈ 1.59 Hz

2. Total Energy of the System

Total mechanical energy: E = Eₖ + Eₚ For a spring-mass system: E = (1/2)mv² + (1/2)kx²
Substitute the initial values: E = (1/2)(1)(1²) + (1/2)(100)(2²) E = 0.5 + 200 E = 200.5 J
Initial kinetic energy: Eₖ(0) = (1/2)(1)(1²) = 0.5 J Initial potential energy: Eₚ(0) = (1/2)(100)(2²) = 200 J
Potential energy at the start ≈ 200 J
Kinetic energy at the start = 0.5 J
Total energy of the system: E = 200.5 J

3. When Is Kinetic Energy 50% of Total Energy?

We want: Eₖ = 50% of E So: Eₖ = E / 2 Since: E = Eₖ + Eₚ then at that moment: Eₚ = E / 2
For spring potential energy: Eₚ = (1/2)kx² Set it equal to half the total energy: (1/2)kx² = E / 2
Substitute k = 100 and E = 200.5: (1/2)(100)x² = 200.5 / 2 50x² = 100.25 x² = 100.25 / 50 x² = 2.005 x = ±√2.005 x ≈ ±1.416 m
The displacement is: x ≈ ±1.42 m

Interpretation

The value ω₀ = 10 rad/s tells us that the system oscillates quite fast, because the spring is stiff and the mass is not large.

The total energy is 200.5 J, and this stays constant during the motion if there is no friction or damping.

At the beginning, almost all the energy is stored in the spring as potential energy because the spring is stretched by 2 m. The initial kinetic energy is very small.

When the mass moves toward the center, spring energy changes into kinetic energy. At the displacement x ≈ ±1.42 m, the kinetic energy becomes exactly half of the total energy.

Final Answers

1. Natural angular frequency: ω₀ = 10 rad/s
2. Natural frequency: f ≈ 1.59 Hz
3. Total energy: E = 200.5 J
4. Displacement where kinetic energy is 50% of total energy: x ≈ ±1.42 m
