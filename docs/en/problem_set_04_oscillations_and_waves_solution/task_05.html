In this exact task both waves have the same k, so the envelope changes with time but does not travel through space.

1. Given waves

y₁(x,t) = A sin(kx − ωt) y₂(x,t) = A sin(kx − (ω + Δω)t)

2. Product form of the resultant wave

Step 1: Add the two waves:
y = y₁ + y₂ = A sin(kx − ωt) + A sin(kx − (ω + Δω)t)
Step 2: Use the identity
sin α + sin β = 2 sin((α+β)/2) cos((α−β)/2)
Step 3: Let
α = kx − ωt β = kx − (ω + Δω)t
Step 4: Compute the average and half-difference:
(α+β)/2 = kx − (ω + Δω/2)t (α−β)/2 = (Δω/2)t
Final product form:
y(x,t)=2A sin(kx−(ω+Δω/2)t) cos((Δω/2)t)

The sine factor is the carrier, and the cosine factor is the envelope.

3. Physical meaning

Carrier wave
This is the fast oscillation:
sin(kx−(ω+Δω/2)t)
It describes the rapid up-and-down motion of the medium.
Envelope
This is the slow amplitude modulation:
2A cos((Δω/2)t)
It tells us when the two waves add strongly and when they nearly cancel.

4. Beat frequency and beat period at x = 0

Beat frequency
f_beats = Δω / (2π)
Beat period
T_beats = 2π / Δω
