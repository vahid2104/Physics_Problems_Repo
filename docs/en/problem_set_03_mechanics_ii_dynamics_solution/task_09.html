Physics • Mechanics • Conservative Field

We are given the potential energy U(x,y) = (k/2)(x² + y²). This page explains the force, equations of motion, type of motion, total energy, and the geometric trajectory with animations.

U(x,y) = k/2 (x² + y²)
See solution Open simulation

Step 1 – Determine the force

For a conservative field, the force is the negative gradient of the potential:

F⃗ = −∇U
Compute the partial derivatives:

∂U/∂x = kx
∂U/∂y = ky
∇U = (kx, ky)

F⃗ = −(kx, ky) = (−kx, −ky)

Conclusion

F⃗(x,y) = (−kx, −ky)

The force always points toward the origin. So it is a restoring force, like a spring in two dimensions.

Step 2 – Write the equations of motion

From Newton’s second law:

m a⃗ = F⃗

Since

a⃗ = (x¨, y¨)

we get

m(x¨, y¨) = (−kx, −ky)

So each coordinate satisfies:

mx¨ = −kx
my¨ = −ky

Simplified form

x¨ + (k/m)x = 0

y¨ + (k/m)y = 0

If we define ω = √(k/m), then:

x¨ + ω²x = 0
y¨ + ω²y = 0

Step 3 – Solve the equations

Each equation is the equation of simple harmonic motion.

x(t) = A cos(ωt) + B sin(ωt)

y(t) = C cos(ωt) + D sin(ωt)

Here A, B, C, D depend on the initial conditions.

Meaning

  • Motion in the x-direction is harmonic.
  • Motion in the y-direction is harmonic.
  • The full motion is the combination of both oscillations.

Step 4 – Determine the type of motion

ω = √(k/m)

Both coordinates oscillate with the same angular frequency. Therefore the particle performs two-dimensional harmonic motion.

Geometric trajectory

  • Usually the trajectory is an ellipse.
  • In a special case, it can be a circle.
  • In another special case, it can be a straight line.

In all cases, the motion is centered around the origin.

Step 5 – Determine the total energy

The total mechanical energy is

E = T + U

The kinetic energy is

T = (1/2)m(ẋ² + ẏ²)

The potential energy is

U = (k/2)(x² + y²)

So the total energy is

E = (1/2)m(ẋ² + ẏ²) + (k/2)(x² + y²)

Important fact

Because the force comes from a potential, the field is conservative.

E = constant

The energy changes form between kinetic and potential, but the total stays the same.

Step 6 – Final answers

F⃗(x,y) = (−kx, −ky)

x¨ + (k/m)x = 0
y¨ + (k/m)y = 0

x(t) = A cos(ωt) + B sin(ωt)
y(t) = C cos(ωt) + D sin(ωt)

ω = √(k/m)

E = (1/2)m(ẋ² + ẏ²) + (k/2)(x² + y²)
Interpretation: The particle oscillates around the origin. The trajectory is usually an ellipse, with special cases of a circle or a straight line.
