1Force

The force is found from the potential energy using:

F(x) = - dU/dx

Now differentiate the potential:

U(x) = (k/2)x² + λx⁴
dU/dx = kx + 4λx³

So the force is:

F(x) = -kx - 4λx³

2Equation of motion

From Newton’s second law:

m x'' = F(x)

Substitute the force:

m x'' = -kx - 4λx³

Move everything to one side:

m x'' + kx + 4λx³ = 0

This is a nonlinear differential equation because of the term x³.

3System of first-order equations

For numerical calculations, we introduce velocity:

v = x'

Then:

x' = v
v' = -(k/m)x - (4λ/m)x³

Why we do this

Computers solve systems of first-order equations more easily than one second-order equation.

What is solved numerically

At each small time step, we update both position x and velocity v.

4Selected parameters

We choose simple parameters:

m = 1,   k = 1,   λ = 0.25

Then the potential becomes:

U(x) = 0.5x² + 0.25x⁴

The force becomes:

F(x) = -x - x³

And the equation of motion is:

x'' = -x - x³

5Initial energy and type of motion

Small energy

Motion stays close to x = 0. The quartic term is weak, so motion looks similar to a harmonic oscillator.

Larger energy

The particle moves farther from the center. The λx⁴ term becomes important and the motion becomes more nonlinear.

Main conclusion

For positive k and positive λ, the potential grows for large |x|, so the motion remains bounded and oscillatory.

E = (1/2)mv² + (k/2)x² + λx⁴

In the phase portrait, low energy gives curves close to ellipses, while higher energy gives more distorted closed curves.

7Final summary

Force: F(x) = -kx - 4λx³
Equation of motion: m x'' + kx + 4λx³ = 0
First-order system: x' = v,   v' = -(k/m)x - (4λ/m)x³

The system is nonlinear because of the cubic force term. For positive k and λ, the motion is bounded. Small energy gives nearly harmonic motion, while larger energy gives stronger nonlinear oscillation.
