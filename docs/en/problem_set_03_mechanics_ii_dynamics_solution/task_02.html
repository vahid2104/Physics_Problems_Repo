Problem 2 – Inclined Plane with Friction

A body of mass m slides down an inclined plane of angle α. The coefficient of kinetic friction is μ. We will find the forces, acceleration, time of descent, final velocity, and check the energy balance step by step.

1. Force Diagram

Three forces act on the body while it slides down the incline:

  • Weight = mg, acting vertically downward.
  • Normal force = N, acting perpendicular to the plane.
  • Friction force = Ff, acting up the plane because it opposes motion.

N = mg cos(α)
Ff = μN = μmg cos(α)

2. Resolve Gravity Into Components

We choose axes:

  • One axis along the plane.
  • One axis perpendicular to the plane.

Then the weight mg is split into two components:

Parallel to the plane: mg sin(α)
Perpendicular to the plane: mg cos(α)

The parallel component pulls the body downward along the plane. The perpendicular component presses the body into the surface.

3. Derive the Acceleration

Step 1: Write the forces along the plane

Down the plane: mg sin(α)

Up the plane: μmg cos(α)

Step 2: Net force along the plane

Fnet = mg sin(α) - μmg cos(α)

Step 3: Apply Newton’s second law

ma = mg sin(α) - μmg cos(α)

Step 4: Divide by m

a = g sin(α) - μg cos(α)
Final acceleration:
a = g [sin(α) - μ cos(α)]

4. Condition for Sliding

For the body to really move downward, the acceleration must be positive:

a > 0
g [sin(α) - μ cos(α)] > 0
sin(α) - μ cos(α) > 0
tan(α) > μ

So the body slides down only if the slope is steep enough compared with friction.

5. Distance Along the Plane

The problem gives the vertical height h, but the body moves along the incline. Let the distance along the plane be s.

sin(α) = h / s
s = h / sin(α)

This is the actual path traveled by the body.

6. Time of Descent

Assume the body starts from rest. Then:

s = (1/2)at²

Substitute:

h / sin(α) = (1/2) g [sin(α) - μ cos(α)] t²

Solve for t²:

t² = 2h / { g sin(α)[sin(α) - μ cos(α)] }
Time of descent:
t = √{ 2h / [ g sin(α)(sin(α) - μ cos(α)) ] }

7. Final Velocity

Use the constant-acceleration formula:

v² = 2as

Substitute the expressions for a and s:

v² = 2 · g[sin(α) - μ cos(α)] · h/sin(α)
v² = 2gh[sin(α) - μ cos(α)] / sin(α)
Final velocity:
v = √{ 2gh[sin(α) - μ cos(α)] / sin(α) }

Its direction is down the plane.

8. Check with Energy Balance

Initial Energy

Ei = mgh

The body starts from rest, so initial kinetic energy is zero.

Final Energy

Ef = (1/2)mv²

At the bottom, the height is zero, so potential energy becomes zero.

Work of friction

Wf = -Ffs
Wf = -μmg cos(α) · h/sin(α)

Energy equation

Ei + Wf = Ef
mgh - μmg cos(α) · h/sin(α) = (1/2)mv²
v² = 2gh[sin(α) - μ cos(α)] / sin(α)
The same formula is obtained, so the result is consistent with the energy balance.

Final Answers

Forces acting on the body:

Weight = mg
Normal force = N = mg cos(α)
Friction force = Ff = μmg cos(α)

Acceleration

a = g [sin(α) - μ cos(α)]

Distance along the plane

s = h / sin(α)

Time of descent

t = √{ 2h / [ g sin(α)(sin(α) - μ cos(α)) ] }

Final velocity

v = √{ 2gh[sin(α) - μ cos(α)] / sin(α) }

Energy check

Result is consistent with energy balance ✅
