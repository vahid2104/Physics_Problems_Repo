Problem 4 – Conservation of Energy

A body falls from a height h₀ without air resistance. We solve the task step by step using a simple explanation and animated presentation.

What do we want to find?

  • The total energy E(h) = T(h) + U(h)
  • The velocity as a function of height v(h)
  • Comparison with Newton’s second law
  • The height where kinetic energy is 75% of total energy
falling body

1. Total energy

The total mechanical energy is the sum of:

  • kinetic energy T(h)
  • potential energy U(h)
So we write:
E(h) = T(h) + U(h)
The kinetic energy is:
T(h) = (1/2) m v(h)^2
The gravitational potential energy is:
U(h) = mgh
Therefore:
E(h) = (1/2) m v(h)^2 + mgh
Since there is no air resistance, the total energy stays constant.

Initial energy

If the body starts from rest at height h₀, then initially v = 0.

E₀ = mgh₀

So at any height h:

(1/2) m v(h)^2 + mgh = mgh₀

2. Velocity as a function of height

Start from conservation of energy:
(1/2) m v(h)^2 + mgh = mgh₀
Subtract mgh from both sides:
(1/2) m v(h)^2 = mg(h₀ - h)
Divide by m:
(1/2) v(h)^2 = g(h₀ - h)
Multiply by 2:
v(h)^2 = 2g(h₀ - h)
Take the square root:
v(h) = √[2g(h₀ - h)]

Therefore, the velocity as a function of height is:

v(h) = √[2g(h₀ - h)]

3. Compare with Newton’s second law

Using Newton’s second law

The only force is gravity:

F = mg

From Newton’s second law:

ma = mg

So the acceleration is:

a = g

Using kinematics

For falling distance s = h₀ - h:

v² = v₀² + 2as

The body starts from rest, so v₀ = 0.

v² = 2g(h₀ - h)

Therefore:

v(h) = √[2g(h₀ - h)]

Both methods give the same result.

v(h) = √[2g(h₀ - h)]

4. At what height is kinetic energy 75% of the total energy?

We want:

T = 0.75 E

The total energy is constant and equal to:

E = mgh₀

So:

T = 0.75 mgh₀

But the kinetic energy also equals the lost potential energy:

T = mg(h₀ - h)

Now set them equal:

mg(h₀ - h) = 0.75 mgh₀
Cancel mg from both sides:
h₀ - h = 0.75 h₀
Solve for h:
h = h₀ - 0.75h₀
Therefore:
h = 0.25 h₀ = h₀ / 4
Total energy = 100%
Kinetic energy = 75%
Potential energy = 25%

The kinetic energy is 75% of the total energy when the body is at height

h = h₀ / 4

Final answers

E(h) = (1/2) m v(h)^2 + mgh
E(h) = mgh₀ (constant)
v(h) = √[2g(h₀ - h)]
h = h₀ / 4

So the body speeds up as it falls, and both methods energy conservation and Newton’s second law give the same answer.

● At 75% kinetic energy, only 25% of the original potential energy remains.
