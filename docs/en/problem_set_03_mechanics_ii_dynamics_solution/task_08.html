Problem 8 – Harmonic Oscillator

Animated explanation of the equation mẍ + kx = 0, including the solution, natural frequency, energy conservation, and phase-space interpretation.

mẍ + kx = 0

1. Solve the equation

We start with the differential equation:
mẍ + kx = 0
Divide both sides by m:
ẍ + (k/m)x = 0
Define the natural angular frequency:
ω₀² = k/m
Then the equation becomes:
ẍ + ω₀²x = 0
The general solution of this standard harmonic oscillator equation is:
x(t) = A cos(ω₀t) + B sin(ω₀t)
where A and B are constants determined by initial conditions.

2. Natural frequency

From the definition
ω₀² = k/m
we get:
ω₀ = √(k/m)
So the natural angular frequency is:
ω₀ = √(k/m)
If we want the ordinary frequency in hertz, then:
f₀ = ω₀ / (2π) = (1 / 2π) √(k/m)

3. Energy as a function of time

The total mechanical energy is the sum of kinetic and potential energy.

Kinetic energy

T = ½ m (ẋ)²

Potential energy

V = ½ k x²

Total energy

E(t) = ½ m (ẋ(t))² + ½ k (x(t))²
Using
x(t) = A cos(ω₀t) + B sin(ω₀t)
its derivative is
ẋ(t) = -Aω₀ sin(ω₀t) + Bω₀ cos(ω₀t)

4. Show that energy is conserved

  1. Start with the total energy: E(t) = ½ m (ẋ)² + ½ k x².
  2. Differentiate with respect to time.
  3. This gives: dE/dt = mẋẍ + kxẋ.
  4. Factor out ẋ: dE/dt = ẋ(mẍ + kx).
  5. But the equation of motion says mẍ + kx = 0.
  6. So dE/dt = 0.
  7. Therefore the total energy is constant in time.
dE/dt = 0 ⟹ E(t) = constant
So energy is conserved.

5. Interpretation in phase space

In phase space, we use the coordinates:
(x, ẋ)
The total energy is:
E = ½ m ẋ² + ½ k x²
Multiply by 2:
2E = mẋ² + kx²
Rearranging gives:
kx² + mẋ² = 2E
This is the equation of an ellipse in the phase plane. So the oscillator moves on a closed curve.
Maximum x → velocity = 0 Maximum velocity → x = 0 Motion is periodic Energy stays constant

7. Final answers

x(t) = A cos(ω₀t) + B sin(ω₀t)
ω₀ = √(k/m)
f₀ = (1 / 2π) √(k/m)
E(t) = ½ m (ẋ(t))² + ½ k (x(t))²
dE/dt = 0 ⟹ energy is conserved
kx² + mẋ² = 2E ⟹ ellipse in phase space
Therefore, the motion is a periodic back-and-forth oscillation with constant total energy, and in phase space it appears as a closed ellipse.
